⚙️Control Panel▾

One settings window for every tool. Turn things on/off, change hotkeys, set colors, and paste your GitHub token.

Open click the ⚙ gear in the map toolbar
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Click the ⚙ gear in the map toolbar to open it.
  2. Each tool has its own section — flip a switch to turn a feature on or off.
  3. Changed something by accident? Click the ↺ arrow next to it to reset just that setting.

Everything it can do

  • One place for every tool's settings — each script registers itself and appears as its own section.
  • Turn any feature on or off with simple switches (and master on/off per tool).
  • Rebind hotkeys — change any shortcut if it clashes with something else.
  • Pick colors & opacity, numbers, and dropdown options for tools that support them.
  • ↺ Reset any single setting back to its default without touching the rest.
  • Tidy layout — sections are grouped and ordered (Outlines, Performance, Hotkeys…) with sub-headers.
  • Holds your GitHub token and shares it with the tools that need it (Map Styler, Issues).
  • Works on the map and the main page at the same time, staying in sync.

Good for

  • Turning off a tool you're not using right now.
  • Fixing a hotkey conflict.
  • One-stop spot to manage everything.

Drawing & measuring on the map

Everyday tools for marking up and measuring the map.

🗺️Map Styler▾

Shows the power-line shielding overlays and checks coverage. The big one most people use daily.

Use Shift+O to toggle
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Press Shift+O to show or hide the overlays.
  2. Open the Outlines section in the ⚙ panel to turn distribution / transmission lines on or off.
  3. Run the Coverage check to drop numbered pins anywhere that's too far from shielding.

Everything it can do

  • Draws shielding overlays & buffers for distribution and transmission power lines, pulled from the site's map files.
  • Coverage Validator — finds spots more than ~200 ft from shielding and drops numbered red pins on each, measuring to the whole line (not just its corners).
  • Per-category control — separate color, opacity, and on/off for distribution vs. transmission.
  • Hide lines you don't care about (right-click) as a personal view — it never changes the real data or affects anyone else.
  • Show-hidden toggle & hidden-line color, plus "clear all my hides for this site."
  • Split multi-segment lines for finer hide control (this one is a real data improvement and is saved for everyone).
  • Flight-path vertex dots toggle for precise editing.
  • Handles map files in several formats (.kml / .kmz, different capitalizations) automatically.
  • Kick fix (Shift+K) to snap things back if the map gets stuck after a refresh.

Good for

  • Confirming a site's missions cover all the power lines.
  • Spotting coverage gaps before a flight.
  • Cutting visual clutter down to just the lines you care about.

Good for

Looking things up

Quickly see details about what's on a site.

🔍Site Setup Tools▾

Right-click anything on the map to see its details. Also adds a site-wide summary table and a stats page with charts.

Use right-click an entity, or Shift+Ctrl+Q
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Right-click any entity for a popup with its name, ID, altitude and notes. Click a number to copy it.
  2. Use Find in Map Entities to jump to it in Percepto's sidebar.
  3. Click SUM (next to ALT/VAL) for the full table; click 📊 for charts and stats.

Everything it can do

  • Right-click inspector for any entity — asset, flight path, no-fly zone, free-flight zone, or marker — showing name, ID, subtype, notes, and validation.
  • Altitudes & elevations in both feet and meters; click any number to copy it.
  • Find in Map Entities — pastes the name into Percepto's own sidebar search and filters to it.
  • SUM panel — a full, floating, resizable table of every entity on the site with search, type-color chips, validation filters, ft↔m toggle, show/hide columns, multi-select, and 3-way column sorting.
  • Click a row to pan the map to it and open its inspector.
  • Export the list (or just your selection) as CSV, TSV, or JSON.
  • 📊 Site Summary stats — donut charts of entity types and validation, keyword breakdowns, auto-detected asset equipment & states, an Asset-Health-by-Equipment matrix, and grouped markers.
  • Copy → Sheets — pastes a fully formatted, colored table straight into Google Sheets or Excel.
  • Inline editing with a pending-changes queue and an Apply step that pushes them through for you.
  • Flight-path segments listed as their own rows for fine detail.

Good for

  • Grabbing an asset name or altitude in one click.
  • Auditing or QA-ing a whole site at a glance.
  • Handing a site summary to your boss in a spreadsheet.

Getting around faster

Less clicking, less waiting, fewer wrong pages.

🧭Defaults▾

Sends you to the right page automatically and tidies up map layers on every site.

Use automatic · settings in the ⚙ panel
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. It just works once installed.
  2. In the ⚙ panel, pick the section you want to land on (e.g. Site Setup).
  3. Switching sites keeps you on the same section instead of bouncing to Data Map.

Everything it can do

  • Land on your section when you open a site from the landing search (you choose which — Site Setup, Data Map, Mission Bank, Insight Manager, or Dashboard).
  • Keep your current section when you switch sites — no more getting dumped on Data Map every time.
  • Auto-redirect dead links — old "past mission" URLs jump straight to the mission log.
  • Auto-tidy map layers on every new site — turn off the ones you never want.
  • Feeder Line on top — flips it off and back on so it draws above the ortho instead of hiding under it.
  • All configurable in the ⚙ panel, including a session-memory on/off.

Good for

  • Always landing on your usual page when you open a site.
  • Stopping the constant "wrong page" detour when switching sites.
  • Skipping the same layer-cleanup clicks on every site.

Power-line map data

For the folks who maintain the shielding lines themselves.

⚡Power Line EditorAdvanced▾

Edit the actual power-line map files right on the map and save changes for everyone.

Open the ⚡ button in the map toolbar
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Click the ⚡ button, then turn on edit mode.
  2. Drag points to move them, draw new lines, or delete lines.
  3. Ctrl+Click a point to branch off it; lines snap to nearby ones.
  4. Save to commit the change for the whole team.

Everything it can do

  • Move vertices — drag any point of an existing line.
  • Add & delete vertices along a line.
  • Draw brand-new lines and delete whole lines.
  • Branch from a vertex — Ctrl+Click a handle to start a new branch off it.
  • Snapping — points snap to nearby vertices and segments (a purple ring shows the snap).
  • Edits both file lines and "green" lines, distribution and transmission.
  • Saves back to GitHub so everyone gets the corrected data.

Good for

  • Fixing a power line that's in the wrong place.
  • Adding a new branch or splitting a line.
  • Keeping the shielding data accurate.

🚩Issues▾

Circle a spot on the map and leave a note about a problem. The whole team sees it, with a status.

Open the 🚩 button in the map toolbar
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Click the 🚩 button and draw a shape around the problem area.
  2. Add a note and a priority.
  3. Track it through Open → Ready → Resolved (it can be re-opened).

Everything it can do

  • Draw an area on the map and attach a note describing the problem.
  • Status workflow — Open → Ready → Resolved / Ignored, and you can re-open.
  • Priority on each issue, with a filter to focus on the urgent ones.
  • Auto-detects affected entities inside your shape and tags them as pills.
  • Shared with the team — synced through GitHub under your own GitHub identity.
  • Approver flow — reviewers get an activity indicator and a "pending my review" shortcut.
  • Comments & history on every issue.
  • Dedicated panel with search, filter, and sort, plus export to Google Sheets.
  • Clickable site name to jump straight to the right site.

Good for

  • Flagging a coverage gap or data error with its exact location.
  • A shared to-do list of site fixes the whole team can see.

Use automatic · settings in the ⚙ panel
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. It works automatically once installed.
  2. In the ⚙ panel, the Performance section lets you toggle what's blocked.

Newer tools still in development — not auto-installed for coworkers yet. Here so you can see what's coming.

🛩️Flight Path Editorin testing▾

Smart flight-path drawing — terrain-following altitudes set automatically, step vertices added where the ground changes, plus on-map edits Percepto can't do natively.

Use open a flight path's editor on the map
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Open a flight path's native editor so its segment numbers show on the map.
  2. Draw it with fewer vertices than you'd normally need — each segment automatically gets its altitude band from the terrain underneath (min = highest ground under it + 100 ft, max = +30 ft).
  3. Where the ground varies more than 30 ft along a segment, it inserts the fewest extra "step" vertices needed to keep the path terrain-safe.
  4. Hold Alt to peek the terrain — yellow dots near your cursor; hover any dot, vertex, or segment number for ground + AGL.
  5. ⛰ Smart-fill path re-does a whole existing path; ⤓ Pre-warm terrain caches a path's elevation so it's instant next time.

Everything it can do

  • Terrain-following altitude bands set as you draw — floor / band width / step sensitivity all adjustable in the Control Panel.
  • Auto-step — splits a segment where the ground varies too much, using the fewest vertices possible (fewer drone stop/starts).
  • Insert a vertex mid-segment by clicking a segment number — Percepto can't do this natively (you'd otherwise rebuild the whole downstream branch).
  • Open path — un-closes a snapped/closed loop (reverses Percepto's "Close Path," which has no undo).
  • Hold-Alt elevation peek — live ground + AGL under any point of the path.
  • Safety rails — every edit is validated and auto-reverts if it would break the path; instant undo on anything.
  • Fast, free terrain — pulls 10 m elevation data that isn't subject to Percepto's rate limits.

Good for

  • Building flight paths with correct, terrain-aware altitudes without hand-typing every band.
  • Adding a vertex mid-path without rebuilding the branch after it.
  • Fixing a closed loop that trapped a segment.
⊕Site Setup Generator — FFZ previewerin testing▾

Auto-builds a starting site setup — inspection free-fly zones around each asset and shielded flight paths — and previews it on the map for you to fine-tune before anything is committed.

Use the ⊕ Generate button in the Asset Inspector summary panel
Tap for details ▾

How to use it

  1. Open the Asset Inspector summary panel and click ⊕ Generate.
  2. It builds a frame of inspection FFZs around each asset (a set standoff off the asset face) plus base→asset flight paths routed along the power-line shielding corridor.
  3. The result previews on the map — adjust standoff, box size, and altitudes to taste.
  4. Commit through the Apply queue, which has dry-run, rollback, and verify rails. Nothing is written until you confirm.

Everything it can do

  • Per-asset inspection FFZ frames — corner-connected side boxes a set distance off each asset, sized to your minimums.
  • Shielded flight paths from the base to each asset, hugging the power-line corridor.
  • DEM-based altitudes on everything it generates.
  • On-map preview first — you tune it, then commit; it never auto-validates.
  • Flags problems — out-of-corridor path segments and assets it couldn't reach.

Good for

  • Standing up a site-setup foundation fast instead of placing every FFZ by hand.
  • Giving yourself a tunable starting point to finetune rather than a blank map.
